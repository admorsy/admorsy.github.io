Race Diversity in Video Games


The gaming industry has been criticized for a lack of racial diversity in its character representation. A large percentage of video game protagonists are white or Asian, while characters of Black, Latino, and Indigenous descent are underrepresented.

Research from the International Game Developers Association (IGDA) indicates that 78% of game developers identify as white or Asian, which mirrors the dominance of these races in character designs and storylines. Here some of the challenges:

Lack of Diverse Characters: Historically, video game characters have been predominantly white or light-skinned. When non-white characters are included, they are often relegated to secondary or stereotypical roles.

Stereotyping and Tokenism: In many cases, characters of color are often presented with exaggerated features or placed in culturally stereotyped roles, perpetuating harmful tropes.

Limited Customization: Even in games where character customization is possible, options for skin tones, hairstyles, and cultural expressions are often limited or poorly implemented.

Industry Barriers: The low representation of game developers from diverse racial backgrounds contributes to this lack of inclusion, as developers' perspectives often influence the stories and characters they create.

Why is it important to address it?

Addressing race in video games matters because media representations influence how people perceive themselves and others. Gaming is a powerful medium that shapes narratives and identities. Providing racially diverse characters helps:

Representation and Empowerment: Gamers from marginalized racial groups are more likely to feel connected to and empowered by characters that reflect their own identities.

Encouraging Empathy: Games with diverse casts can foster empathy and understanding among players, allowing them to experience stories and perspectives outside their own.

Combating Racism: Offering varied racial representations can counteract harmful stereotypes and normalize diversity, helping to deconstruct ingrained racial biases.

For my Fall ‘24 semester, I took two classes about video games. One is about game theory and what “play” is and how it’s important to everyone. The second class was more technical introducing programming concepts using C# language.

I wanted to practice what I’m learning in an actual project, so I decided my capstone project will be a prototype of a simple platformer game that has an option to customize the main character in the game. Of course, this concept can extend and be as complex as more work is done on the game development but for the purposes of this project, I wanted to keep it simple. Just five options that represent skin colo rs, which in turn can represent the idea of race diversity.

Where?

My plan for the game is to be mainly online on websites like itch.io. There could be further adaptations for different operating systems like MacOS and Windows if the game gains interests.

Who?

My target audience is those interested in indie games. Hopefully the game reaches the racial groups it is intended to, and provides them a sense of belonging that should be there in the first place. The game is also for teachers and educators to incorporate in their activities with young students to develop their self-confidence and empathy towards one another. Plus later levels of the game should have more puzzles and advanced scenarios that can be a fun way to develop student thinking.

When?

This was my original timeline that I made to plan the process:

Concept and Planning


Define the Game Concept: Decide on the theme, story, and main mechanics. This includes the customization options for the character's appearance.

Game Design Document (GDD): Write a GDD to outline gameplay, story, levels, controls, and any unique features.


Prototyping


Basic Mechanics: Implement core gameplay mechanics like movement, jumping, and collisions.

Level Layout: Create basic level layouts to test how the player will interact with platforms and obstacles.

Customization Mechanic: Prototype the character customization feature, especially as it relates to the theme.


Asset Creation

Character and Environment Art: Design and create character sprites, backgrounds, and platform assets. Use software like Photoshop or Illustrator for 2D art, then import it into Unity.

Sound and Music: Develop or source sound effects and background music to match the theme. (TBD)


Development and Integration


Gameplay Mechanics: Code character abilities, enemies, obstacles, and game physics.

User Interface (UI): Design and implement HUD elements like score, health, and customization options.

Character Customization: Implement UI elements and underlying code for customizing the character’s appearance.


Level Design


Detailed Level Design: Build A level that becomes progressively challenging, introduce new mechanics gradually, and fit the game’s story and theme. (TBD)

Testing: Test game level(s) to ensure they’re engaging and fair.


Testing and Iteration


Playtesting: Have players test the game to gather feedback on mechanics, difficulty, and enjoyment.

Bug Fixing: Identify and resolve bugs, especially those related to player movement, physics, and customization.

Polish: Refine animations, transitions, and UI to improve the game experience.


Submit/Publish


Publish the game: Make sure the game runs smoothly on all target platforms. (TBD)
