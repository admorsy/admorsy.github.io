This ongoing personal project began in 2013 as an exploration of creating meaningful, personalized gifts. It originated with a piece I designed for a close friend, featuring his name rendered in Arabic and arranged in a calligraphic style. At the time, I had no formal training in Arabic calligraphy, but I used digital tools to emulate its visual qualities and create something both distinctive and personal. As my friend is Polish, the use of Arabic script added a unique cultural and emotional dimension that made the piece especially meaningful.

Since then, I have continued to create similar works as birthday gifts for non-Arabic-speaking friends, each centered around their names in stylized Arabic forms. This practice led me to pursue a deeper understanding of the discipline. I acquired traditional calligraphy tools, including a pen and ink, and began studying Arabic calligraphy by hand, along with its historical context and stylistic variations.

My long-term goal is to recreate my digitally produced pieces using traditional techniques. As I refine my skills in composing single-word works, I plan to expand into more complex compositions involving multiple words and phrases—such as quotes, proverbs, or short poetic texts.


Design Process

Each piece begins with writing the name in Arabic using a typeface that reflects the characteristics of traditional calligraphy—most often inspired by the Thuluth style, which I find particularly expressive. The lettering is then converted into vector shapes, allowing for precise, individual manipulation of each form.

From there, I explore various compositions, experimenting with the arrangement of shapes to achieve a balanced and visually engaging result. In some cases, I introduce decorative elements to occupy negative space, adding intricacy and depth to the overall design.

Color selection is a key part of the process. I test different palettes to identify combinations that enhance the composition, and occasionally incorporate the recipient’s preferred colors to further personalize the work. For the background, I often apply textured effects—such as washed or distressed finishes—which contrast effectively with the solid forms of the lettering.

Once the composition, color palette, and textures are finalized, I make subtle adjustments to brightness and contrast to refine the overall presentation. The completed piece is then printed on high-quality paper and framed, or produced on canvas, depending on the desired finish.
