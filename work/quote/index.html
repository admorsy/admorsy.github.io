This project was about experimenting with hand lettering and typography to write a quote that I find inspiring. I wanted to explore how to visually represent the meaning of the quote through the design of the letters and their arrangement. The quote I chose is "Judge not, for everyone has a story to tell." I'm not sure who said it first, but it's a powerful reminder to be empathetic and open-minded towards others, as we never know what they might be going through.


Design Process

The process started with brainstorming and sketching different ideas for how to visually represent the quote. I experimented with various styles of lettering. I used a pencil to draw the outline of the letters in one of the sketches. For the other two, I used a brush and black ink. Afterward, I scanned the sketches, cleaned them up, and converted them into vector format using Adobe Illustrator. I played around with the arrangement of the words, trying to find a composition that would be visually interesting and convey the message effectively. After several iterations, I settled on three different layouts; one for each sketch/scan. Next step was to add some color to the words and background. I aimed at choosing different color combinations for each poster to stand out. Last task was to finalize the design and prepare it for printing. Overall, it was a fun and rewarding project that allowed me to explore different techniques and styles of lettering and typography, while also conveying a meaningful message through the design.
