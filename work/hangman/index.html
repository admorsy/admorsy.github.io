Hangman | Video Game
Unity game development C# animation
background

Hangman is a small game I developed in Unity as part of my early exploration of game development. Before this project, most of my work in Unity focused on experimenting with individual mechanics and learning the engine—building small systems that could later be reused in future games.

While my main interest lies in platformer games, I chose to create a version of the classic Hangman game as a manageable project that I could complete from start to finish. The goal was to focus on implementing core gameplay logic, structuring a simple game loop, and finishing a polished, playable experience.

To add personality to the otherwise familiar gameplay, I introduced a short animation sequence that plays at the end of each round.


Design Process

The gameplay follows the traditional Hangman format, where the player attempts to guess a hidden word before making too many incorrect guesses. The main design addition was the animated stick-figure character, which reacts differently depending on the outcome of the game.

If the player successfully guesses the word, the rope disappears and the character raises his arms in celebration. If the player loses, a platform beneath the character opens, leaving him suspended from the rope.

These small animations were designed to give the game a sense of feedback and humor, while reinforcing the result of the round in a visual way.

From a development perspective, the project served as a way to bring together several systems I had previously experimented with in Unity, including basic game logic, simple animation, and user interaction. Completing this game helped me better understand the process of turning isolated mechanics into a finished interactive project.
