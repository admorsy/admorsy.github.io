Millennium Art Gallery is a framing store located in the heart of Philadelphia, PA. The store specializes in custom framing services for art collectors, photographers, and artists. The gallery offers a wide range of framing options, including traditional, modern, and contemporary styles, as well as a variety of matting and glazing options. The gallery also features a curated selection of art prints and posters for sale, showcasing the work of local and international artists.

When I first moved to Philadelphia, I had the opportunity to work on a branding project for Millennium Art Gallery. The business had been established for several years but was looking to refresh its brand identity for better exposure and to attract new customers. As a graphic designer, I was tasked with creating a new logo, color palette, and branding materials that would reflect the gallery's values and aesthetic.


Design Process

For the logo design, I wanted to incorporate visual elements that clearly convey what the store offers even if the name is not visible. The primary visual elements I chose are two elements: a picture frame and a paintbrush. The old logo used red and black as the primary colors which made the branding look too serious and formal. I wanted to change the color scheme to be more modern and vibrant and to convey a sense of creativity and friendliness.

So I decided to use yellow as the primary branding color along with black and white as colors for the logotype. The yellow color represents creativity, optimism, and energy, which are all qualities that align with the gallery's mission to inspire and support artists and art collectors. The black and white colors provide a clean and modern look that complements the yellow and helps to create a strong visual contrast.

In addition to the logo and color palette, I also designed a range of branding materials for Millennium Art Gallery, including business cards, flyers, and signage. In addition, I designed and built a new website for the gallery that tied all the branding materials together. I helped with the website content, as well, by using images of framed artwork and photographs to showcase the gallery's services.

Overall, the branding project for Millennium Art Gallery was a great opp ortunity to apply my design skills to a real-world project and to help a local business refresh its brand identity.
